Current Draft · Opening Scene

The river had been here longer than memory.

Its waters slipped over countless stones, each one shaped by time into something smooth enough for the current to trust. Sunlight broke across the surface in shifting patterns that refused to stay still, scattering bright fragments across the water before carrying them away again.

Along the bank, tall stems bent gently toward the current. Broad leaves shimmered as they turned, revealing different shades with each passing breath of air. Tiny shapes skimmed across the water so lightly they left only circles that vanished almost as soon as they appeared. Above them, feathered bodies drifted effortlessly between towering branches, disappearing into the light before emerging somewhere else entirely.

The world never seemed to stop moving.

Everywhere there was something becoming something else.

A droplet gathered at the end of a leaf until it could no longer hold itself.

It fell.

The river accepted it without notice.

Small feet wandered along the edge of the water, pausing far more often than they continued. Every polished stone deserved to be turned over. Every ripple carried something worth watching. Every unfamiliar shape invited another question.

Several steps ahead, larger footprints followed the river with patient certainty.

The distance between them stretched.

Then closed.

Then stretched again.

Neither seemed concerned.

This was simply how they traveled.

The child lingered beside a stone the water had worn into a perfect curve. They traced its edge before letting it fall back into the current with a quiet splash.

They hurried to catch up.

Another few moments passed.

Then they stopped.

Not because something called.

Not because something moved.

Because something...didn't belong to the rhythm of everything else.

The river flowed.

The leaves turned.

The small drifting bodies above continued their silent dance.

But just beyond the reach of the current...

Four stones stood balanced upon one another.

The child stared.

The world seemed suddenly quieter.

The water still moved.

The leaves still swayed.

Yet all of it faded behind the strange stillness of those four stones.

They looked as though they had always been there.

They looked as though they could not possibly have arrived there alone.

The child looked toward the one walking ahead.

Only now had the footsteps stopped.

The larger figure turned.

Its gaze settled first on the child.

Then followed the child's eyes to the stones.

A gentle smile.

"What do you see?"

The child opened their mouth.

Nothing came.

Not because there was no answer.

Because there wasn't one yet.

They looked back at the stones.

Then at the river.

Then at the hand waiting patiently several steps away.

No one hurried them.

No one explained.

The child stepped closer.

The ground yielded softly beneath careful feet.

One hand reached toward the lowest stone.

Cool.

Solid.

Unmoving.

Another rested upon the one above it.

Each carried a different weight.

A different shape.

Yet together they had found balance.

The child's eyes wandered across the riverbank until they settled on a stone resting half beneath the water.

Not remarkable.

Not perfect.

Simply the one that felt right.

They lifted it carefully.

Water slipped from its surface and returned to the river without protest.

For a long moment, the child simply held it.

It was heavier than it looked.

Its edges pressed gently against their hand.

Slowly...

The child raised it toward the top of the stack.

The world seemed to wait with them.

The river still flowed.

But somehow more quietly.

The leaves still turned.

Yet none of them seemed to move.

Even the drifting shapes above felt suspended, as though the moment had asked everything around it for patience.

The stone reached the top.

It touched.

It leaned.

The child's hand hovered beneath it, ready but unwilling to interfere.

A heartbeat.

Another.

The smallest shift.

Then...

The weight settled.

The stones accepted one another.

Balance returned.

The river found its voice again.

Water laughed against the bank.

The leaves whispered overhead.

Somewhere deeper in the trees, a distant call echoed before fading into the morning.

The child smiled without realizing they had.

Something inside had quietly found its place.

"Come along," the parent called.

The child looked once more at the stack, then turned and hurried toward the waiting hand.

Behind them, the river continued on its endless journey.

Only one thing had changed.

There was one more stone than before.

Revision Notes

  • Preserve the quiet identity of the scene.
  • Do not reveal the child's biology yet.
  • Do not explain the meaning of the stones inside the story.
  • Future edits should maintain the rhythm of suspense, balance, release, and return.
