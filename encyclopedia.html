Prologue Entry

Status: Canonical development direction established. Final prose remains draft material.

The prologue follows one ordinary non-human conscious being across three life phases: childhood, adulthood, and elderhood. The purpose is to let the reader experience the value of one life before learning how physically different that being may be from humanity.

The opening scene begins in childhood beside a river. The child walks with a parent, notices a balanced stack of stones, approaches it, adds one more stone, and returns to the parent. The action is ordinary but becomes the first expression of contribution and accumulation.

Opening Scene Elements

  • Setting: a riverbank or shallow river path.
  • Primary figures: a child and a parent, not yet named or biologically defined.
  • Central object: a stack of stones balanced near the water's edge.
  • Central action: the child adds one stone to the existing stack.
  • Central feeling: curiosity giving way to quiet satisfaction and release.
  • Parent role: patient trust rather than instruction or explanation.

Current Canon Status

The first story lens is not yet biologically defined. The opening should allow the reader to emotionally connect before understanding what the being is.

The word "alien" should not be used by the narrator. The narration should describe beings and creatures by what they do and how they are experienced, not through Earth-centered categories.
