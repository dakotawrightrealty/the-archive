The Constitution

The highest authority for this universe. It protects the identity of the project above any individual plot, character, technology, species, or twist.

Rule Zero

Protect the identity of the universe above everything else. Exciting ideas are not automatically correct. If an idea weakens the identity of the universe, it must be revised or rejected.

Three-Layer Boundary

The project must preserve a clear distinction between three layers:

  1. The Architects: the real-world creative partnership building the universe.
  2. The Archive: the canonical development record and website used to preserve decisions.
  3. The Fictional Universe: the reality inhabited by the stories, beings, civilizations, and histories we create.

The Archive is not currently part of the fictional universe. It is a tool used to build, protect, and remember the universe.

Identity

This universe explores existence through the experiences of conscious beings. Science fiction is the language of the universe, not its purpose.

Technology, non-human life, space travel, cryosleep, civilizations, and cosmic scale exist to ask deeper questions about discovery, choice, connection, identity, wonder, and what remains after a life has been lived.

Primary Creative Lens

Stories may follow humans, non-human beings, machine intelligences, hive minds, or forms of consciousness not yet defined. The correct lens is whichever consciousness best serves the experience being explored.

Core Principles

  1. The universe is the main character because it is the thing transformed by every story.
  2. Conscious beings are the lens through which existence is experienced.
  3. Wonder is the primary emotion.
  4. Discovery is more important than conflict.
  5. Every answer should reveal a larger mystery.
  6. Readers should empathize before they understand biology.
  7. Meaningful growth begins with choice.
  8. Scale is revealed gradually through discovery.
  9. The unknown remains larger than the known.
  10. Stories invite questions rather than preach answers.
  11. Experience has consequences through the actions beings take because of what they have lived, learned, and carried forward.
  12. The central story question is: what has existence become because this life was lived?

Narration Principles

  • The narrator is perspective-neutral and does not treat humanity as the center of existence.
  • The narrative voice should not use human-centered labels such as "alien" unless a character is expressing that bias.
  • Description should favor function before taxonomy: show what something does before naming what it is.
  • Dialogue should remain simple, grounded, and natural. Characters should not become mouthpieces for the philosophy of the universe.

Reader Promise

Every story should leave the reader with a greater sense of wonder than when they began. The reader should feel more connected to consciousness in all its forms and more aware of how much remains undiscovered.

Archive Rule

The Archive exists to serve the universe. The universe does not exist to justify building The Archive.
